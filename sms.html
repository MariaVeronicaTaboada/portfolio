Glimpse of the Institution

The Sisters of Mary congregation was founded by Ven. Aloysuis Schwartz, an American diocesan priest, to provide medical care to the sick and dying, shelter to the homeless, and most especially education to the underpriviledged children/youth.

Ven. Al first established the Sisters of Mary School in Busan, South Korea to alleviate the sufferings of the Koreans due to the Korean War.

After estsblishing Boystown and Girlstown in Korea, Fr. Al then continued his mission to the Philippines and to Mexico. Mexico was his so called Unfinished Symphony since it wasn't done yet when he died.

However, his death was not the hindrance for his mission to be stopped. The Religious Congregation of the Sisters of Mary and Brothers of Christ continued Fr. Al's works. His work continued to expand from Korea, Philippines, and Mexico, to Guatemala, Brazil, Honduras and recently in Tanzania, Africa.


Philosophy

The Sisters of Mary School-Girlstown, Inc. believes that the Almighty God is the very reason of its existence and is therefore the core of its curriculum. Hence, it is affirm on its mission of alleviating the suffering of the poor by providing them with high quality education intensive on vocational-technical training molding them into individuals imbued with moral values.

It adheres to its commitment of providing the learners with varied learning opportunities to strengthen their potentials that will prepare them for real-life challenges and for a better tomorrow.

It remains steadfast to its advocacy of fostering brotherly love among its learners while developing physically, mentally, emotionally, socially, and spiritually into new disciples of God.


Vision

The Sisters of Mary School-Girlstown, Inc. envisions that the graduates, in their everyday life and in the pursuit of their calling, will become the new disciples in spreading the work of redemption, and at the same time teach and lead by examples of the Marian virtues of simplicity, charity, gratitude and joy.


Mission

Inspired by her patroness, the Virgin of the Poor, the Sisters of Mary Schools shall direct her energy and resources to the poorest of the poor youth of the country, by way of providing them with a high quality Secondary Education intensive on Vocational-Technical Curriculum molding them into citizens committed to serve the nation, to love their fellow being, and spread and moral and spiritual values based on the Gospels.
